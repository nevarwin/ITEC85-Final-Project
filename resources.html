Recommended Books

Practical Reverse Engineering by Bruce Dang

Discover the world of reverse engineering through the expertise of Bruce Dang, former senior software security officer at Microsoft. Practical Reverse Engineering offers practical insights into analyzing hardware and software without access to source code, helping information assurance professionals understand how reverse engineering can enhance cybersecurity strategies.

Cybersecurity & Cyberwar by Allan Friedman

Delve into the realm of cyber warfare and its impact on modern society with Cybersecurity & Cyberwar. Allan Friedman provides real-world examples and answers important questions about the internet's role in warfare. Gain a deeper understanding of cyber threats and learn strategies to combat and prevent cyber warfare.

The Practice of Network Security Monitoring: Understanding Incident Detection & Response by Richard Bejtlich

Learn from Richard Bejtlich, an experienced cybersecurity expert, about network security monitoring and its significance in detecting and responding to intruders. The Practice of Network Security Monitoring offers step-by-step instructions, open-source tools, and practical insights to help organizations build and run effective network security monitoring operations.

The Art of Computer Virus Research & Defense by Peter Szor

Explore the world of computer viruses with Peter Szor, a renowned computer security researcher. The Art of Computer Virus Research & Defense provides valuable knowledge on virus analysis, spread mechanisms, and effective defense strategies. Enhance your understanding of computer viruses and develop strategies to identify and defend against them.

Practical Malware Analysis: The Hands-On Guide to Dissecting Malicious Software by Michael Sikorski

Prepare for the evolving threat of malware with Practical Malware Analysis. Michael Sikorski guides information assurance professionals through the process of safely analyzing and dissecting malware. Learn effective techniques to stay ahead of the latest malware and protect your network from malicious attacks.

The Art of Software Security Assessment by Mark Dowd

Master the art of software security assessment with insights from leading security consultants in The Art of Software Security Assessment. Mark Dowd provides comprehensive guidance on uncovering security flaws in software applications and auditing their effectiveness. Strengthen your ability to develop secure software and address potential vulnerabilities.

The Art of Memory Forensics by Michael Hale Ligh

Gain expertise in memory forensics, a cutting-edge field in digital crime investigation, with The Art of Memory Forensics. Michael Hale Ligh shares advanced techniques for analyzing computer memory to solve cybercrimes. Stay ahead of the evolving threats and develop skills in this high-demand area of information assurance.

These resources, authored by industry professionals with diverse experiences, provide valuable insights and knowledge to help information assurance professionals tackle the growing threats faced by organizations and individuals in today's digital landscape. Whether you're seeking to enhance cybersecurity strategies, understand cyber warfare, analyze malware, assess software security, or investigate digital crimes, these resources offer valuable guidance to strengthen your information assurance skills.

Recommended Online Courses

Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)

The CISSP certification from the cybersecurity professional organization (ISC)² ranks among the most sought-after credentials in the industry. Earning your CISSP demonstrates that you’re experienced in IT security and capable of designing, implementing, and monitoring a cybersecurity program.

Visit CISSP Certification for more information.

Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA)

This credential from IT professional association ISACA helps demonstrate your expertise in assessing security vulnerabilities, designing and implementing controls, and reporting on compliance. It’s among the most recognized certifications for careers in cybersecurity auditing.

Visit CISA Certification for more information.

Certified Information Security Manager (CISM)

With the CISM certification, also from ISACA, you can validate your expertise in the management side of information security, including topics like governance, program development, and program, incident, and risk management.

Visit CISM Certification for more information.

CompTIA Security+

CompTIA Security+ is an entry-level security certification that validates the core skills needed in any cybersecurity role. With this certification, demonstrate your ability to assess the security of an organization, monitor and secure cloud, mobile, and internet of things (IoT) environments, understand laws and regulations related to risk and compliance, and identify and respond to security incidents.

Visit CompTIA Security+ Certification for more information.

Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)

Ethical hacking, also known as white hat hacking, penetration testing, or red team, involves lawfully hacking organizations to try and uncover vulnerabilities before malicious players do. The EC-Council offers the CEH Certified Ethical Hacker certification. Earn it to demonstrate your skills in penetration testing, attack detection, vectors, and prevention.

Visit CEH Certification for more information.

GIAC Security Essentials Certification (GSEC)

This certification from the Global Information Assurance Certification (GIAC) is an entry-level security credential for those with some background in information systems and networking. Earning this credential validates your skills in security tasks like active defense, network security, cryptography, incident response, and cloud security.

Visit GSEC Certification for more information.
